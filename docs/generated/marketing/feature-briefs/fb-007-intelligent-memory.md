# Feature Brief: Intelligent Memory

## One-Liner

Iris learns how you work and remembers what matters.

## What It Is

Iris builds a persistent understanding of your communication patterns, preferences, and past interactions. The AI assistant remembers conversations across sessions, learns from every correction you make, and personalizes itself to the way you actually use email — not the way some product team imagined you would.

## Who It's For

- Professionals who are tired of re-explaining context to AI tools every time they open them
- Power users who want their email client to get smarter over time, not just run the same generic model forever
- Privacy-first users who want adaptive AI without feeding their data to a cloud learning pipeline

## The Problem It Solves

Every AI email assistant on the market today has the same flaw: amnesia. Close the chat, and it forgets everything. Correct a misclassification, and it makes the same mistake tomorrow. You're perpetually training a tool that never retains what you teach it.

Cloud-based assistants could theoretically learn — but that means shipping your behavioral data to someone else's servers. The result: AI that's either forgetful or invasive. Neither is acceptable.

## Solution

Iris remembers past conversations, learns from your corrections, and personalizes over time — all on your machine. No cloud round-trips, no data leaving your hardware, no privacy compromise. The AI gets better because you use it, not because a company harvests your behavior.

## Key Benefits

- **Cross-session memory**: Your AI assistant remembers past conversations. Pick up where you left off — days, weeks, or months later — without repeating yourself.
- **Adaptive classification**: Every correction you make trains the system. Miscategorized an email? Fix it once. Iris adjusts and gets it right next time.
- **Persistent preferences**: Your communication patterns, priorities, and working style are retained. Iris gets meaningfully smarter the more you use it.
- **Entirely local**: All learning data stays on your machine. No behavioral profiles uploaded to the cloud. No one else benefits from your corrections but you.

## How It Works

When you interact with Iris — correcting a classification, chatting with the AI, or flagging a priority — those interactions are stored locally. The next time the AI processes your email or answers a question, it draws on that history. Classifications become more accurate. Chat responses become more relevant. The assistant builds a working model of how you think about your email, and it does so without any data ever leaving your device.

Background processing ensures this learning happens reliably. Every email is processed, every correction is recorded, and nothing slips through the cracks — even if you close the app mid-sync.
