# Feature Brief: Context Autocomplete

## One-Liner
Start typing a reply and Iris completes your thought — using the actual conversation as context.

## What It Is
Iris brings AI-powered autocomplete to your compose window that understands the thread you're replying to. As you type, suggestions appear that are grounded in the specific names, topics, dates, and details already discussed in the conversation. Accept a suggestion with Tab, dismiss with Escape — it stays out of your way until you want it.

## Who It's For
- **Primary:** High-volume email users who write many replies per day and feel the cumulative cost of composing from scratch each time
- **Secondary:** Anyone who struggles to phrase responses quickly, especially when catching up on a complex thread

## The Problem It Solves
Writing email replies is repetitive, especially when you're referencing information that's already in the thread. You re-read the conversation, pull out the relevant details, then rephrase them in your own response. It's low-value work — you're not thinking, you're transcribing. Even quick replies take longer than they should when you're context-switching across many threads.

## Key Benefits
- **Faster replies:** Complete sentences and paragraphs in seconds instead of composing word by word
- **Context-aware suggestions:** Completions reference the actual thread — names, dates, topics — not generic filler
- **Non-intrusive UX:** Suggestions appear inline and disappear instantly — Tab to accept, Escape to dismiss, or just keep typing
- **Reduced cognitive load:** Less effort spent retrieving details from the thread; Iris pulls them into your draft for you
- **Consistent and accurate:** Suggestions reflect what was actually said in the conversation, reducing errors from misremembering details

## How It Works (Simplified)
When you open a reply window, Iris reads the full thread and builds a context model of the conversation — who said what, when, and about what topics. As you type, the AI generates continuations that are grounded in that context, surfacing relevant names, dates, and discussion points from earlier in the thread. Suggestions appear as ghost text after your cursor. Press Tab to accept the suggestion, or simply keep typing to ignore it. The feature is always on in compose and requires no configuration.
