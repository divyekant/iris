# Response Time Patterns

## One-Liner
See exactly how fast you and your contacts reply to each other — and what that means for how you communicate.

## What It Is
Response Time Patterns measures the reply cadence between you and any contact. For both sides of the conversation, it shows average reply time, fastest reply, and slowest reply. Visual speed indicators — fast, medium, or slow — make the data immediately readable. This gives you a factual, objective picture of responsiveness across your email relationships.

## Who It's For
Sales professionals tracking prospect engagement, managers monitoring team responsiveness, anyone managing a relationship where timing matters — and professionals who want an honest look at their own response habits.

## The Problem It Solves
Responsiveness is one of the clearest signals in any professional relationship, but most people only have a gut feeling about it. Is this prospect going cold, or do they always reply slowly? Am I actually fast to respond, or do I just feel like I am? Response Time Patterns replaces guesswork with real data, so you can make decisions based on what's actually happening.

## Key Benefits
- **Know where you stand.** See your own average reply time, objectively, for any relationship.
- **Read engagement signals.** A contact who usually replies in minutes but has gone quiet is telling you something.
- **Time your follow-ups better.** Know when a contact is likely to respond — and when it's too soon to send again.
- **Spot outliers.** Fastest and slowest reply times reveal the full range of a contact's communication style.
- **Simple at a glance.** Speed indicators (fast/medium/slow) make the data readable without studying numbers.

## How It Works (Simplified)
Open any contact or conversation in Iris and view Response Time Patterns. Iris calculates reply times across your shared email history and presents average, fastest, and slowest figures for both you and the contact. Color-coded speed indicators tell you immediately what the pattern means.
