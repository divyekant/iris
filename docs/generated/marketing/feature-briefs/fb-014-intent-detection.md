# Feature Brief: Intent Detection

## One-Liner
Iris reads every email and tells you exactly what it wants from you — before you open it.

## What It Is
Iris automatically classifies every incoming email by intent the moment it arrives. Whether it's an action request, a question, an FYI, a scheduling ask, a sales pitch, a social message, or a newsletter — you see a clear badge right in your inbox. No tagging, no rules, no setup.

## Who It's For
- **Primary:** Knowledge workers and managers who receive high email volume and need to triage fast
- **Secondary:** Anyone tired of opening emails only to discover they required nothing from them

## The Problem It Solves
You open 40 emails before lunch and only 8 needed a response. The rest were updates, newsletters, or sales pitches — but you had no way to know that without reading each one. Triage is a tax you pay constantly, and it adds up.

## Key Benefits
- **Instant triage:** Know what needs your attention and what doesn't without opening a single email
- **Zero setup:** Intent detection is on by default — AI does the work the moment messages sync
- **Prioritized focus:** Filter your view to action requests or questions only, and clear the noise
- **Reduced context-switching:** Stop interrupting deep work to check if an email was important
- **Better inbox awareness:** See patterns in what's coming in — too many FYIs? Too many pitches?

## How It Works (Simplified)
Every email that arrives in Iris is automatically analyzed by AI and assigned one of seven intent categories: action request, question, FYI, scheduling, sales pitch, social message, or newsletter. A compact badge appears next to the subject line in your inbox so you can scan and triage at a glance. No rules to write, no labels to configure — it runs continuously in the background on every message.

## Suggested Messaging
- "Know what every email wants from you — before you open it."
- "Stop guessing. Iris labels every email by intent, automatically."
- "Action required. FYI. Question. Iris handles triage so you don't have to."
